## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027)

**The $0.99 charge IS the fraud signal.**

Cardholders default-treat sub-$1 charges as noise — forgotten
subscriptions, app-store re-auths, donation rounds, tier
downgrades. Standard per-card velocity rules at issuer banks
default-tune for amount thresholds (above $50, above $200,
cross-border at any amount, etc.) that miss the sub-$1 testing
band entirely. Card Testing weaponizes both defaults
simultaneously: the test charge is invisible to the cardholder's
attention threshold AND invisible to the per-card velocity rule
threshold, while being plainly visible at the cross-card
aggregate level if anyone has built a rule for it.

The diagnostic protocol shifts from "watch for unauthorized
large transactions" to "watch for the unauthorized small ones,
because they precede the large ones by days or weeks." The
cardholder's single most leveraged action is to dispute every
unfamiliar small charge regardless of amount — but this requires
education that does not currently exist in mainstream Caribbean
cardholder onboarding. The institution's single most leveraged
control is a cross-card velocity rule (N+ distinct cards from
the same issuer hitting one merchant within M minutes) which is
structurally different from the per-card-amount rules most
issuers run.

The technique completes the picture started by Silent Alarm
(v25.7.0.27): F1008.001 closes the customer's detection surface
on a specific account; F1043 closes both the customer's and the
issuer's detection surfaces on a population of accounts. The
two together explain why fraud loss numbers continue to compound
at Caribbean retail banks despite per-card monitoring being
mature — the techniques are tuned against the wrong dimensions.

## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller:** A teller will never directly observe Card Testing
  in the moment — the testing happens at e-commerce merchants
  during Positioning and the cardholder does not present at the
  teller window during this phase. The teller's leverage is
  upstream: when a customer reports a card-not-recognized small
  charge during a routine teller interaction (deposit, balance
  check, statement query), the teller's diagnostic prompt should
  treat the small charge as a Card Testing signal and trigger an
  immediate card-reissue + dispute filing rather than the routine
  "we'll mark it for monthly statement review" response. The
  trainee level: small unrecognized charges are not statement
  noise; they are Positioning evidence.

- **Analyst:** Card Testing fits the analyst lens precisely
  because it is the analyst-side dimension shift this animation
  teaches. The analyst is the role that sets velocity-rule
  parameters and reviews fraud-engine output. The animation
  surfaces the gap between per-card-amount rules (which fire on
  Stage 7's $1,247 transaction successfully but contribute
  nothing during Stages 3-5) and cross-card-merchant rules
  (which would have fired in Stage 3 and prevented Stages 5-7
  entirely). Analyst-facing learning outcome: review your
  velocity-rule taxonomy; ensure cross-card-aggregate rules
  exist and are tuned for sub-$1 amounts.

- **SOC:** Pure client-side animation release, no backend / no
  schema / no seed. New scene file imports; entry in
  ANIMATION_MAP; audio version bump. Risk surface limited to
  the F1043 sub-technique sidebar route — no other technique's
  rendering is affected. The 4-actor convention is maintained
  matching every other Phase 1 multi-actor scene and Silent
  Alarm.

- **Executive:** The executive takeaway is the supply-chain
  framing. A single stolen card moves through three distinct
  fraudster operations (breach buyer → testing crew → resale
  buyer → full-fraud operator) over ~28 days. The testing crew
  earns $1,544 net on a $554 lot purchase (280% return) by
  validating which cards still authorize and reselling the
  validated subset. The economic structure tells leadership why
  Card Testing is not a marginal nuisance — it is the
  margin-generating step that makes the rest of the supply
  chain economically viable. Disrupting Card Testing at the
  issuer level, via cross-card velocity rules, structurally
  raises the cost of every downstream fraud category that
  depends on validated card data.
