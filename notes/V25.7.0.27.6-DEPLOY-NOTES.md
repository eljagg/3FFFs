## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027 — methodological)

**The play-through is the orientation; the manual scrub is the
investigation.**

Two distinct trainee actions deserve two distinct affordances on
the same visualization. When a trainee first opens "How attackers
wait," they don't yet know what to look for — they need a guided
play-through that walks them through the 30-day attack chronology,
showing each day's attacker action and signal as it appears. That
play-through is orientation: "here's the shape of the attack,
here's where the signals are." Once oriented, the trainee wants
to investigate — drag back to Day 8, see what changed, toggle a
control, replay just that segment. That's the manual scrub:
investigation.

Before this release the visualization was investigation-first —
no orientation. Trainees opened it and were confronted with a
day-0 timeline and no clear narrative. The Play button is the
missing orientation affordance. Every interactive visualization
that represents a temporal or sequential process should have both:
a play-through to orient newcomers, and direct manipulation for
the experienced.

This is also why technique animations (F1067, F1073, F1008.001,
etc.) all default to a play-through model — they're orientation
content. The kill-chain grid (Reconnaissance) is the exception
because it's already orientation-shaped (linear stages, clear
left-to-right flow) without needing playback. Positioning's
30-day timeline and two-views split-screen are NOT orientation-
shaped on their own; they need the Play affordance to become so.

## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller:** Tellers opening "How attackers wait" or "The
  disguise" can now hit Play and watch the Account Rental attack
  unfold without needing to know how to drag a scrubber or which
  controls to toggle first. The trainee experiences the patient,
  legitimate-looking nature of Positioning as a play-through —
  Day 0 account opening, Day 1 device added, Day 8 e-delivery
  changed, Day 11 first incoming, etc. — building intuition for
  why detection at this stage matters before the trainee starts
  investigating individual signals.

- **Analyst:** Analysts who want to inspect specific signal-to-
  control mappings can still drag the scrubber and toggle controls
  manually — the moment they interact, playback pauses. Play is
  additive, not replacing. The Day-counter and "X of N controls
  active" readouts give precise positional context during both
  manual investigation and automated playback.

- **SOC:** Pure client-side change to two existing component
  files. No backend, no schema, no seed, no Framework.jsx,
  no sidebar component changes. New state and effect are fully
  scoped within each component. setInterval is properly cleaned
  up via useEffect cleanup. No memory leaks. Existing telemetry
  events (viz_scrubbed_to_day, viz_control_toggled,
  viz_scenario_picked) are preserved; three new event types
  added (viz_played, viz_paused, viz_replayed).

- **Executive:** A demo walkthrough now flows like this — open
  Framework, click Positioning, expand techniques, click "How
  attackers wait," hit Play, narrate the 30-day attack as it
  plays. Then click "The disguise," hit Play, narrate the
  unravel-one-detection-at-a-time reveal. The CRO sees the
  pedagogy without having to learn the controls. This is the
  same demo flow that worked for the technique animations
  (F1067 OSINT Profiling, F1073 IVR Discovery) — extending it
  to the tactic-level visualizations means every Framework page
  surface in the demo is now a play-through experience.

## Verification — 90 seconds

### Step 1 — "How attackers wait" plays through (30 sec)

1. Navigate to Framework → Positioning (FA0001)
2. Expand the techniques toggle
3. Click "How attackers wait" card → slide-out opens
4. Top of the visualization, observe: **▶ Play button (left)**,
   "Day 0 of 30" counter (right)
5. Click ▶ Play. Observe:
   - Button changes to "❚❚ Pause" with accent-color background
   - Day counter counts up: 1, 2, 3 ... 30 (about 10 seconds total)
   - Attacker actions appear in the lanes as their day arrives
   - Signals appear as their day arrives
6. When it reaches Day 30, button shows "↻ Replay"
7. Click ↻ Replay → starts again from Day 0

**Pass:** the 30-day Account Rental attack plays through, attacker
and signal lanes populate as days pass.

### Step 2 — Manual scrub still works mid-playback (15 sec)

1. With "How attackers wait" still open, click Play
2. After a few seconds, drag the scrubber backward
3. Observe: playback pauses immediately, button shows ▶ Play
   again, day cursor jumps to where you dragged
4. Toggle a defender control on → its expected catch day shows
5. Click Play to resume from current day

**Pass:** manual scrub pauses playback; existing investigation
flow is preserved.

### Step 3 — "The disguise" plays through (20 sec)

1. Close, click "The disguise" card → slide-out opens
2. Top of the visualization, observe: **▶ Play button (left)**,
   "0 of N controls active" counter (right)
3. Click ▶ Play. Observe:
   - Each control activates one-by-one (about 1.5s apart)
   - Each activation surfaces its hidden signal in the bank-side
     view
   - Counter updates: "1 of N", "2 of N", "N of N"
4. When all controls are active, button shows "↻ Replay"
5. Click ↻ Replay → all controls reset, playback starts again

**Pass:** disguise unravels one detection at a time, hidden
signals surface progressively.

### Step 4 — Manual control toggle pauses playback (10 sec)

1. Reset by clicking Replay then immediately clicking a control
   manually
2. Observe: playback pauses, manual toggle takes effect, control
   count reflects the manual change

**Pass:** manual takes precedence over automation.

### Step 5 — Console clean + telemetry firing (15 sec)

1. Open dev tools → Network tab, filter for "viz"
2. Click Play, Pause, Replay across both visualizations
3. Confirm telemetry events firing:
   `viz_played`, `viz_paused`, `viz_replayed`, plus existing
   `viz_scrubbed_to_day`, `viz_control_toggled`, `viz_scenario_picked`
4. Check Console — no red errors

**Pass:** clean.

## Honest accounting

Non-goals — explicit:

- **No backend / schema / seed changes.** Visualization configs
  are unchanged. The same example data drives both manual and
  automated play.
- **No Framework.jsx changes.** Tactic-page rendering from .27.4
  unchanged.
- **No TacticVisualizationSidebar.jsx changes.** Slide-out
  structure from .27.5 unchanged. The Play button lives inside
  the visualization component itself, where it belongs (not in
  the sidebar wrapper).
- **No KillChainGrid changes.** Kill chain is already orientation-
  shaped (linear left-to-right stages); doesn't need a Play button
  per the pedagogical-insight reasoning above.
- **No DefenseEvasionTwoViews changes in this release.** It's
  the same `two_views` kind as PositioningTwoViews, but uses a
  separate component file. Symmetry update deferred to a follow-up
  release once .27.6 verifies as the right pattern. (If Omar
  prefers, the same change can land in DefenseEvasionTwoViews in
  a v25.7.0.27.7 hotfix using the same diff.)
- **Playback cadence is fixed** at 333ms/day for the timeline
  and 1500ms/control for two-views. No speed control exposed in
  this release. Could be added later if pedagogically useful, but
  not on the critical path.
- **Scenario tabs not auto-advanced.** If a future viz config
  has multiple examples, Play only plays the currently-selected
  example's full sequence. User picks the next example manually.
