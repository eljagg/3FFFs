# v25.7.0.15 — Audio narration system (Web Speech API; muteable; per-message dialogue)

Cross-cutting feature release. Adds spoken audio to animation
dialogue using the browser's built-in Web Speech API. Pedagogical
intent: tellers and customer-service staff at Caribbean banks hear
actual fraudster voices when customers report incidents — they need
to recognize the voice pattern (cadence, scripted authority language,
specific phrases) as the fraud signature. Reading "Officer Reid says
'don't hang up — the bank may be compromised'" is one thing. Hearing
it spoken in a confident calm authority-projecting voice is another.

## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027)

**Trainees should hear the fraud, not just read about it.** The decision
to add audio is grounded in two things:

1. **Recognition pedagogy.** Bank staff who recognize the vishing kill
   chain in customer dispute reports recover faster than those who
   treat it as user error. Recognition is auditory in real-world
   incident response — the customer says "the man on the phone
   said..." and the staff member needs to recognize the voice pattern.
2. **Realism.** The training principle locked v25.7.0.12+ ("real-world
   training, not sheltered") extends naturally to audio. Watching a
   fraudster's words in text on screen is a sanitized version of what
   the customer actually experienced. Hearing it spoken with calm
   authority is closer to the real exposure.

The mute control respects trainees who need to focus on visual content
or who are in environments where audio is impractical. Default unmuted
so first-watch trainees encounter the realism by design.

## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller:** Hears the actual call structure. When a customer says
  "the man kept telling me not to hang up and not to call my bank,"
  the teller has heard that exact pattern in training and recognizes
  it instantly. Dispute categorization improves; recovery window
  widens.
- **Analyst:** The OTP system voice ("never share this code with
  anyone, including JNCB staff") plays in the same SMS audio that the
  fraudster's "that warning is for external scams" reframe plays
  against. The juxtaposition is more striking when both are audible.
- **SOC:** The IVR menu prompts in IVR Discovery (F1073) demonstrate
  what the attacker actually heard during reconnaissance — useful
  context when SOC reviews IVR system anomaly reports.
- **Executive:** Audio adds visceral weight to the institutional
  argument. A board member who hears "Officer Reid" speak in a calm
  professional voice for 60 seconds across the Vishing animation
  internalizes "this is sophisticated; customer vigilance alone
  cannot defeat this" more deeply than reading the script.

## What ships in this release

### Architecture

New module: `audioNarration.js` (~210 lines).
- Wraps `window.speechSynthesis` (browser-native Web Speech API).
- Voice profile system: `authority`, `fraudster`, `victim`, `victimMale`,
  `system`, `investigator`, `narrator`. Each maps to pitch + rate
  settings that distinguish voices.
- `useNarration()` React hook returning `{ speakMessage, stopAll, isSupported }`.
- Defensive against browser quirks (Chrome's async voice list, etc.).
- Migration path documented for future pre-generated audio (option B):
  scenes can declare `audio: { src: '/audio/X.mp3' }` and the engine
  will play files when available, fall back to TTS otherwise.

All three engines integrated:
- **MultiActorSequenceAnimation** — per-message audio (each message in
  a stage may have an `audio` field). Speeches scheduled to play
  sequentially across the stage's duration. Speaker icon (🔊) renders
  on the actively-speaking message.
- **ProcessAnimation** — per-stage audio. Stage `audio` field plays on
  stage entry. IVR Discovery uses this for the IVR menu prompts.
- **TimelineThresholdAnimation** — per-stage audio. Functional but
  inert for current Structuring scene (no dialogue); future case-review
  animations can opt in.

### Mute control

A 🔊/🔇 toggle button appears in the playback bar of all three engines.
- Default state: unmuted (audio plays)
- Persists within the session (does not reset between stages)
- When muted, no speech is queued; when unmuted, audio resumes from
  the next stage entry
- Hidden if browser doesn't support speechSynthesis (rare)
- Respects all playback controls — pause cancels audio, scrub backward
  cancels and replays from new stage, restart resets

## Voice profile mapping

Browser TTS picks the actual voice from whatever is installed on the
trainee's OS/browser. The pitch and rate settings differentiate the
voices to the trainee's ear:

| Profile | Pitch | Rate | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| `authority` | 0.9 | 0.92 | Fraudster as Officer Reid / FID / NCB Security — calm, lower, slower |
| `fraudster` | 1.0 | 1.0 | Fraudster as themselves (recruitment, internal) |
| `victim` | 1.05 | 0.98 | Female customer (Beverly, Tanya) |
| `victimMale` | 0.95 | 0.98 | Male customer (Devon) |
| `system` | 1.1 | 1.05 | Bank/telco automated systems (OTP SMS, IVR) |
| `investigator` | 1.0 | 0.98 | Bank/telco fraud-ops in callback dialogue |

These differences are modest. Caribbean accents are not natively
available in any browser's voice library as of v25.7.0.15. Quality
ceiling is "British or American English-accented robot reading text" —
not "indistinguishable from real human." If trainee feedback indicates
the quality is insufficient for the realism objective, v25.7.0.16+ can
add pre-generated audio files via ElevenLabs (option B from the design
discussion). The architecture supports this with zero engine refactor —
scenes just add `src: '/audio/...'` to the audio object.

## Honest accounting

Five notes:

1. **Voice quality is the core risk of option A.** Browser TTS is a
   commodity tool. Trainees may find the voices too robotic for the
   realism objective. If feedback indicates this, the migration path
   to option B (ElevenLabs pre-generated audio) is documented in
   `audioNarration.js` and architecturally supported with zero engine
   refactor.

2. **No Caribbean accent is available.** This is a real limitation.
   The closest approximations are "Microsoft David" / "Alex" / "Zira"
   on most systems. Caribbean trainees may notice the mismatch.
   Audio still teaches the recognition pattern (cadence, structure,
   specific phrases) which transfers across accents.

3. **Audio does not play on first user interaction in some browsers.**
   Modern browsers require a user gesture before allowing audio — the
   ▶ Play button click is the gesture. Audio should work from that
   point onward. If a trainee opens an animation but never clicks Play
   (only steps through with arrows), audio may be blocked on the
   first stage. Stepping through to a second stage typically works.

4. **The 🔊 audio toggle is a session-only state.** It doesn't persist
   across page refreshes or to other animations. If a trainee mutes
   Vishing then opens Phishing, Phishing starts unmuted. Persistence
   across animations would require localStorage which is forbidden in
   Anthropic artifacts but allowed in production — could be added
   in a future polish release if requested.

5. **Vishing has the strongest audio coverage (16/23 messages).**
   This is the right priority. The Vishing animation is where the
   audio investment pays off most — vishing is dialogue-heavy, and
   the script is the entire pedagogy. SIM Swap and Phishing have
   lighter coverage because their kill chains are mostly silent
   technical events (port-out at the carrier, SMS interception, http
   flows) — narrating those events would be over-narration. The
   audio coverage matches what would actually be heard in the real
   incident.

The audio narration system shipped here is the right architectural
foundation. Whether v25.7.0.16 author the next animation with audio
from day one, or first ship a v25.7.0.15.1 polish to address
quality feedback, depends on what you find when you test.
