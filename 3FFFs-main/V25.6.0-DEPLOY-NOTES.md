## Interactivity Design (OBS-018, locked this release as permanent practice)

Per the four user levels: Frontline/Teller, Fraud Analyst, SOC/Cyber Team, Risk Manager/Exec.

### Frontline / Teller

**No Frontline/Teller-facing change in v25.6.0.** CBEST is governance and SOC territory; Tellers don't engage with SC013/SC014 in their primary learning path, and the role pills on these scenarios do not include `teller`.

**Forward-pointer:** v25.6.1 (CBEST FrameworkPhase nodes — ISS-013) creates a structural opening for a role-conditional CBEST card on the Frameworks page. A Teller who lands on the CBEST framework via curiosity could then see a "How this matters to you" tooltip that surfaces the helpdesk-vishing TTP in SC014-S1 as the same social-engineering vector that targets branch staff on Friday afternoons. v25.6.0 itself does not deliver this — it removes the misinformation that would otherwise propagate forward.

### Fraud Analyst

**What changes:** SC013 narrative now uses BoE-correct deliverable names. The analyst can distinguish three distinct CBEST deliverables that previously collapsed into one:

- **Targeting Report** (CBEST 2024e) — what attackers can learn about us
- **Threat Intelligence Report** (CBEST 2024f) — who would plausibly target us, and how
- **Penetration Test Report** (CBEST 2024h) — what the testers actually achieved

**What they see:**
- Stage 3 (Procurement) narrative now references "Threat Intelligence Report (CBEST 2024f)" instead of the fictional "Threat Modelling Report"
- Stage 4 (Validation) narrative now describes the three-hour Validation workshop with regulator + PTSP — closer to the actual BoE process
- Procurement Concept chip displays **"CBEST Accreditation"** (renamed from "CREST Accreditation")
- Stage 6 (Closure) narrative now references the **PT Report** and **D&R Capability Assessment** as the two distinct closure deliverables, with each finding tagged to its source document

**What they learn:** The analyst now has the precise vocabulary required to participate in a real CBEST conversation — distinguishing TI deliverables from PT deliverables, knowing which BoE document specifies each. When they see a real Targeting Report or TI Report cross their desk, they recognise it.

### SOC / Cyber Team

**What changes:** SC014 no longer carries a fictional 4-hour BoE notification clock. The S6 decision still requires immediate escalation, but the framing is now grounded in real BoE language: secrecy obligations under Implementation Guide §3.2.2 and §5 — judgement-based, immediate, not a specific countdown.

**What they see:**
- Stage 6 correct-answer text: "Notify the regulator immediately per CBEST secrecy obligations (Implementation Guide §3.2.2 / §5)" — replaces the fictional "4-hour window"
- Stage 6 rationale: explicit citation of the BoE Implementation Guide section on secrecy compromise notification
- Stage 6-CONSEQUENCE: reframed around delayed notification as a finding under secrecy obligations (not against a specific clock that doesn't exist)
- S2/S4 rationales now correctly identify the **Penetration Test Report** and **D&R Capability Assessment** as the closure deliverables that name SOC findings — instead of the fictional "IDR Report"

**What they learn:**
1. Removes a false procedural anchor (the 4-hour clock) that could mis-fire when the analyst encounters real CBEST or DORA conversations. DORA has its own incident reporting clocks; CBEST does not.
2. Names the actual deliverable that captures D&R findings — so when a CCSAM-led D&R Capability Assessment lands, the SOC analyst recognises the document and understands its place in the closure pack.
3. Reinforces that immediate notification is a regulatory necessity rooted in firm-side secrecy obligations, not a paperwork deadline.

### Risk Manager / Exec

**What changes:** SC013 procurement and closure stages now reference the correct regulator (the regulator / BoE — not "Sector Cyber Team") and correct accreditation gate (CBEST-accredited). Engagement duration calibrated to BoE's published 9-12 month average.

**What they see:**
- Scenario summary opens with realistic engagement duration: "Your job for the next nine to twelve months..."
- Stage 3 (Procurement) Concept chip displays "CBEST Accreditation" with BoE-aligned summary that names BoE as accreditation authority and CREST as membership co-requirement
- Stage 3-CONSEQUENCE narrative now correctly identifies the BoE as the issuer of the accreditation certificate (Provider Gamma's lapse refers to the BoE certificate, not a CREST one)
- Stage 6 (Closure) narrative correctly identifies the two closure deliverables and ties each finding to its source document

**What they learn:**
1. **Procurement gate:** when signing a CBEST procurement contract, the gate to verify is **CBEST accreditation** issued by the Bank of England — checked against the register published on the CREST website. Verifying CREST accreditation alone is necessary but not sufficient.
2. **Capacity planning:** Board-level resource planning calibrated to the right timeline (9-12 months end-to-end, not 6).
3. **Closure governance:** the firm receives two distinct closure deliverables (PT Report + D&R Capability Assessment), each signed off separately. Remediation prioritisation is the firm's responsibility, supervised — not delegated to the regulator.

## Honest reflections on tradeoffs

**The Concept node ID stays `CONCEPT-CREST-ACCREDITATION`** — graph identity is stable, no edge migration. Trade-off: URLs and debug surfaces still say CREST. Acceptable for hotfix; flagged for a future schema-migration release.

**No schema-level CBEST FrameworkPhase nodes yet** — that's ISS-013, scoped for v25.6.1. Until then, SC013 stages reference phase numbers in their narratives (Phase 1.3 Scoping, Phase 2.3 Validation, etc.) but there's no clickable phase chip wedge yet. v25.6.0 makes the narrative correct so v25.6.1 can build the wedge on solid foundations.

**Per-framework concept summary for CBEST** — that's ISS-014, scoped for v25.6.2. The CBEST-side `APPEARS_IN_FRAMEWORK` summary on `CONCEPT-CREST-ACCREDITATION` is updated in this release (since add-sc013.js writes it), but other concepts that have `pending: true` for CBEST will be filled in by v25.6.2.

**Risk of regression:** zero scenarios deleted, zero stages deleted, zero edges removed. All edits are property updates on existing nodes. Migration preservation checks will report 0 deltas — same pattern as the v25.5.2 idempotent re-run that just succeeded.
