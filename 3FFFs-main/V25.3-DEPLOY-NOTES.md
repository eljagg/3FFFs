# v25.3 Deployment Notes — SC011 + SC012 (Frameworks Phase 3)

**Two new analyst-tier scenarios that extend the AASE arc beyond Planning
into Execution and Closure.** Server-only release. No client changes. Same
ConceptSidebar + concept-lookup + AASE phase chip behavior the v25.1 client
already supports — these scenarios just plug into that surface with new
content.

## What's new

**SC011 — "Risk Management Mid-Exercise — five live calls"** (HIGH severity,
~$8.5M estimated loss)

Six primary stages + four consequence branches. The analyst plays the
Exercise Director three weeks into a live AASE at First Caribbean Trust.
Each stage is a real-time decision pulled from section 7.3 of the AASE
guidance:

1. Evidence of prior compromise discovered during execution → Test/Halt
2. Public-exposed RCE in the customer portal mid-test → secrecy vs. customer safety
3. SOC about to escalate to JCF Cybercrime Unit → Working Group expansion
4. Real production outage (treasury-impacting) caused by Attacker activity → halt-and-disclose
5. Concession creep — fourth concession request → cumulative-impact discipline
6. Final reflection on the Test/Halt cadence across the engagement

Concepts tested: `CONCEPT-TEST-HALT-MODEL` (×3), `CONCEPT-EXERCISE-SECRECY`,
`CONCEPT-ESCALATION-PATH`, `CONCEPT-CONCESSION`. All stages map to
`AASE-PHASE-EXECUTION`.

Consequence branches on Stages 1, 2, 3, 4 — the highest-stakes wrong
answers each get a dedicated outcome stage showing what real damage looks
like. Stage 5 (concession creep) and Stage 6 (final reflection) have no
consequence branches; the wrong-option rationales handle the lesson.

**SC012 — "Reading the Defence Report — what the evidence really says"**
(MEDIUM severity, ~$2.5M estimated loss)

Five primary stages + one consequence branch. Different scenario shape —
the analyst is post-exercise reading a fictional Defence Report from First
Caribbean Trust's Blue Team and a parallel Exercise Report from the
Provider, and making interpretation calls. The narrative arc: across the
five stages the analyst progressively recognises that the Defence Report
is too positive — the SOC was tipped off — and has to surface that to the
CISO and ultimately the Board.

Stages:
1. Detection times unusually consistent across attack sophistications
   (statistical signal of compromised secrecy)
2. EDR coverage gap framed as licensing problem when it's really process
3. SWIFT message exfiltration achieved by Provider but not mentioned in
   Defence Report (a complete miss the Defence Report itself doesn't flag)
4. Translating findings into top-3 remediation priorities for the Board
5. How candid should the Board memo be? — political tension on the
   secrecy finding

Concepts tested: `CONCEPT-EXERCISE-SECRECY` (×2), `CONCEPT-CRITICAL-FUNCTION`
(×3). All stages map to `AASE-PHASE-CLOSURE`. One consequence branch on
Stage 1 (the most important finding to get right).

## Rollback

`git revert HEAD && git push` reverts the code. To remove SC011/SC012 from
the graph:

```cypher
MATCH (sc:Scenario) WHERE sc.id IN ['SC011', 'SC012']
MATCH (sc)-[:HAS_STAGE]->(st:Stage)
DETACH DELETE st;
MATCH (sc:Scenario) WHERE sc.id IN ['SC011', 'SC012']
DETACH DELETE sc;
```

## Authoring notes for future scenarios

The template that produced SC010, SC011, SC012 is now stable:

- Severity HIGH for execution-phase decisions, MEDIUM for analytical /
  interpretive scenarios, LOW reserved for orientation-style scenarios
  (none yet)
- 5-7 primary stages per scenario, 1-3 consequence branches on the
  highest-stakes wrong answers
- Each primary stage carries `aaseConcept` and `aasePhase` IDs for the
  graph wiring
- Narrative voice: tight, present-tense, named characters from First
  Caribbean Trust (CISO, COO, CIO, Head of Risk, etc.) — keeps the
  fictional bank concrete without naming a real institution
- Consequence branches are 2-option ("what was the lesson?") with one
  right-framing and one deflection — they are short reflection moments,
  not full multi-option stages
- Wrong-answer rationales must explain the *seductive logic* of the wrong
  pick, not just dismiss it. The reader needs to recognise their own
  reasoning to learn from it.

This template can produce SC013+ in future releases without further
infrastructure work.
