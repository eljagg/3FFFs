## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027)

**ATO variant diagnosis matters because remediation differs.**

F1018 at the parent-category level is a fraud-shape, not an
actionable technique. The five-plus sub-technique variants
(.001 Password Reset, .002 Credential Stuffing, .003 Session
Cookie Theft, .004 OAuth Token Hijack, etc.) each have completely
different kill chains, completely different remediation paths,
and completely different recovery prospects. Caribbean banks
that report ATO at the parent-category level only inherit the
institutional false sense that "we fixed ATO last year."

The pedagogical core: variant resolution matters more than
parent-category resolution. The parent technique exists to
ORGANIZE the variants; the variants exist to DRIVE the
engineering work. Treating them as the same is treating fraud
as a brand rather than a problem.

This animation is the first Phase 1 piece authored from the
defender perspective. Every prior Phase 1 animation (F1081, F1088,
T1451, F1018.001, T1621, F1072) told a single specific kill chain
from the ATTACKER'S perspective. F1018 has the opportunity to
teach the DEFENDER'S perspective — the analyst's diagnostic
triage protocol, the per-variant tracking that surfaces
institutional trends, and the budget-allocation argument that
flows from accurate variant data.

For Caribbean banking trainers, the operational takeaway is:
when a customer disputes transactions and reports an ATO loss,
the analyst's first three questions matter more than which
audit-log entries they pull. Customer interview FIRST, audit
log SECOND, time correlation THIRD. The combinatorial answer
identifies the variant; the variant drives correct remediation
routing.

## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller:** When a customer reports ATO at the front desk, the
  intake interview is the first diagnostic instrument. Use the
  three-question protocol: any phishing/vishing/push artifacts in
  the past 30 days; what the customer recalls of any unusual
  account activity; whether they have used public WiFi or shared
  a device recently. Document each answer separately. The
  analyst will use this interview as the customer-side input to
  variant diagnosis. A weak intake produces a misdiagnosed case.

- **Analyst:** Run the standardized diagnostic protocol on every
  ATO dispute, regardless of how obvious the variant seems.
  Customer interview before audit log. Three questions. The
  variant determines the remediation team routing AND the
  customer-facing remediation advice. Misdiagnosis means the
  customer gets wrong advice and the institution gets wrong
  signal about which variant is spiking.

- **SOC:** Maintain per-variant fraud rate dashboards. Track
  F1018.001, .002, .003, .004 as separate fraud categories with
  monthly volume and value metrics. Spikes in one variant
  trigger targeted institutional fixes. Without per-variant
  tracking, "ATO is up" is the only signal, and remediation
  budget gets allocated to whichever variant generated the most
  recent loud case rather than the actually-spiking one.

- **Executive:** The naive control "treat all ATO disputes the
  same way" is presented and explicitly countered. Variant-blind
  triage is the most common operational defect in Caribbean
  banking fraud ops as of 2026. Per-variant dashboards plus
  standardized triage protocol convert the ATO dispute queue
  from a recovery-and-reimbursement workflow into an
  institutional learning loop. The cost calculus favors the
  small process investment (standardized protocol, per-variant
  dashboards) at any realistic incident rate.

## Honest accounting

Three notes:

1. **Different pedagogical structure than prior Phase 1
   animations.** F1018 teaches the defender perspective rather
   than the attacker kill chain. The trade-off: less narrative
   tension (no single victim's loss is the dramatic core), more
   institutional substance (the diagnostic protocol, the
   per-variant dashboards, the budget-allocation argument). For
   a parent canonical technique, the institutional substance is
   the right pedagogical job. The .001-.004 sub-technique
   animations carry the kill-chain narrative; F1018 carries the
   classification-and-response framework.

2. **First release authored at 2.0 w/s preferred budget.** Per
   the v25.7.0.19 finding (you reported audio sounded better
   without any engine change; most likely cause was Stage 7's
   16-second stretch giving 36 words more headroom than other
   dialogue stages). I trimmed Karelle's interview questions
   (visible in message labels, not narrated) to preserve the
   60-second total runtime while keeping all audio at the softer
   cadence. If audio quality continues to feel improved at 2.0
   w/s, the budget will lock in as the new default for v25.7.0.21+.

3. **Parent-vs-sub pedagogical distinction.** F1018 (this
   release) and F1018.001 (v25.7.0.16) are both Initial Access
   ATO animations but answer different pedagogical questions.
   F1018.001 answers "what is the Password Reset attack chain
   and how does it complete." F1018 answers "how does an analyst
   tell which ATO variant happened, and why does the
   classification matter." Both belong in the F3 framework. A
   trainee who watches F1018.001 sees one specific kill chain
   in detail; a trainee who watches F1018 sees the diagnostic
   landscape. The two complement rather than duplicate.
