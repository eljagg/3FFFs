## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027)

**Caribbean banking phishing has a recognizable Caribbean signature.**
Jamaican phishing campaigns aren't generic global phishing — they
target NCB / JNCB / Scotia JM / CIBC by name; reference J$ amounts;
spoof local bank phone numbers; arrive from 876-prefix numbers or
international SMS gateways; use Smishing Triad-style kits that
specifically support Caribbean geo-targeting. **Tellers and analysts
who recognize the Caribbean signature catch attacks earlier — and
recognize the pattern in customer dispute reports.**

This animation teaches the recognition skill specifically calibrated
to what Caribbean bank staff actually see. Real characters (Mrs.
Beverly Williams), real institution (NCB Jamaica), real case anchor
(NCB J$47.5M phishing, April-June 2022), real kit context (Smishing
Triad's Lighthouse, documented via Silent Push research March 2025),
real institutional decision (NCB suspended SMS alert system in
October 2023 rather than secure the messaging channel).

## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller:** When Beverly walks in next morning to dispute the J$2.4M
  transfer, the teller is the first responder. The animation teaches
  the recognition pattern — Beverly's description of the SMS, the
  typosquat domain, the urgency framing, the 30-minute deadline — so
  the teller can categorize this as smishing-victim-scenario and
  escalate to fraud ops with the right context. Without this
  recognition, the dispute gets miscategorized and Beverly's recovery
  window narrows further.
- **Analyst:** The AML analyst reviewing Beverly's transfer pattern
  sees the J$2.4M to a 30-day-old beneficiary account at 4:20 PM,
  53 seconds after a login from a Russian IP. Velocity rules and
  login-anomaly monitoring catch this in real-time IF properly
  configured. The animation shows what those rules look like in action
  — and what happens when they aren't configured.
- **SOC:** The SOC owns login-anomaly monitoring and velocity rule
  configuration. The animation maps each detection control to real
  monitoring system features. SMS alert system is a customer-facing
  detection capability — the SOC's responsibility to keep operational.
- **Executive:** Stage 7 surfaces NCB's October 2023 decision to
  suspend SMS transaction alerts entirely as the institutional
  response to the smishing epidemic. This is a real public executive
  decision that the animation makes visible. Drives policy
  conversations about what controls to invest in vs. remove, and
  whether "the customer's responsibility to be vigilant" is a
  defensible institutional posture.

### The 4 actors

1. **Fraudster crew** — Smishing Triad kit operator, initial state "active"
2. **Mrs. Beverly Williams** — NCB customer, initial state "unaware",
   transitions through "targeted" → "compromised" → "aware"
3. **NCB Jamaica** — bank login + transfer systems, initial state "silent",
   transitions to "investigating" at the end
4. **Mule beneficiary chain** — 23 dispersion accounts (matching the
   real NCB J$47.5M case dispersion network), initial state "silent",
   transitions to "active"

### The 7 stages (41s at 1×)

| # | Stage | Duration | What |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Smishing kit purchase | 5s | Fraudster acquires Smishing Triad-style "Lighthouse" kit via Telegram; cost a few hundred USD |
| 2 | Bulk SMS broadcast | 5s | 50,000 messages broadcast to 876-prefix Jamaican numbers |
| 3 | The message arrives | 6s | Beverly receives smishing SMS at 4:17 PM with 30-minute urgency |
| 4 | The clone site | 7s | Beverly clicks; pixel-perfect NCB clone harvests credentials |
| 5 | OTP harvest | 6s | Real OTP captured and replayed within 60s; Russian IP authenticates |
| 6 | Transfer cascade | 6s | J$2.4M moves out in 47s to Beneficiary #1; cascades through 23-account dispersion |
| 7 | The aftermath | 6s | Without SMS alerts (NCB suspended October 2023), Beverly discovers loss next morning |

### The 5 detection controls (4 real + 1 naive)

1. **Customer info-hygiene training (recognize-and-report)** — catches
   stages 3-4 if customer recognizes the smishing pattern
2. **Real-time login-anomaly monitoring** — catches the
   impossible-travel signal at stage 5
3. **Outbound transfer velocity rules** — would have held the J$2.4M
   transfer for callback verification at stage 6
4. **Active SMS alert system** — would have given Beverly a 5-minute
   window to call NCB and stop the cascade. NCB suspended this in
   October 2023 — the animation makes that institutional decision
   visible
5. **NAIVE: Customers should know not to click suspicious links** —
   surfaces the publicly-stated "customers are exposing themselves"
   framing as the failed institutional posture it is

### The 5 hidden signals

1. Typosquat domain (ncb-secure.live vs jncb.com) — revealed by customer hygiene control
2. Urgency + verify-or-lose-account language pattern — revealed by customer hygiene control
3. OTP entered from different IP within 60 seconds — revealed by login anomaly control
4. J$2.4M transfer to new beneficiary within 60s of login — revealed by velocity rules
5. No customer notification due to suspended SMS alerts — revealed by SMS alert system control
