## Four-lens block (OBS-018)

- **Teller lens:** Tellers see one transaction at a time. The animation
  teaches that even though they only see ONE deposit, that deposit may
  be part of a structuring pattern visible only across the broader
  timeline. The teller's response — escalating an unusual single
  deposit to compliance — is the input the analyst uses to assemble
  the bigger picture.
- **Analyst lens:** Most direct. The trainee IS the analyst in the
  case-review framing. Two accounts flagged for review, multi-angle
  visualization, the trainee's job is to determine which is structuring
  and write up findings. This is exactly what AML analysts do daily.
- **SOC lens:** SOC perspective is on monitoring system configuration:
  which detection rules surface these patterns, which miss them, which
  generate too much false-positive noise. The detection-control toggles
  in the animation map to real monitoring-system features (aggregation
  rules, behavioral baseline rules, CDD-vs-activity, peer-group analysis).
- **Executive lens:** Stage 5 final headline emphasizes the actual
  job: pattern recognition across time, channels, and customer baseline.
  Drives policy conversations about monitoring system investment and
  compliance staffing.

## Pedagogical insight (OBS-027)

**Structuring is invisible because no single transaction looks wrong.**
Every individual deposit is below the J$1M POCA Section 101A threshold.
Every individual deposit may match patterns of legitimate cash-heavy
customers. The pattern only exists if you look ACROSS time, ACROSS
channels, and AGAINST the customer's established behavioral baseline.

**A compliance analyst's actual job isn't transaction-by-transaction
review — it's pattern recognition across time and across customer
baselines.**

### The 5 detection controls (4 real + 1 naive)

1. **Aggregation across channels** — catches Trevor's channel fragmentation
2. **Behavioral baseline deviation** — Marcia has 4 years; Trevor has none
3. **CDD currency vs. transaction reality** — Marcia matches; Trevor doesn't
4. **Peer-group analysis** — Trevor 99th percentile among similar new accounts
5. **NAIVE: Block all sub-threshold cash deposits** — illegal AND ineffective; would block Mrs. Edwards's legitimate vendor deposits AND fail to catch sophisticated structurers

### The regulatory framework (stage 5)

- **POCA Section 101A** — J$1M cash transaction limit
- **POCA (MLP) Regulations 2007** — TTRs filed quarterly; STRs/SARs filed within 15 days
- **Financial Investigations Division (FID)** — Designated Authority, goAML platform
- **Failure to report** — itself an offence under POCA; institution AND Nominated Officer can be liable
